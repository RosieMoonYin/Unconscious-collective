which is a Christianised westernised time,
which is clock time.
For me dreams are circular time,

but perhaps, through that lense life doesnt make sense, we just think it does.

The truth is language has limits when describing our complex reality - by using a lot of words we can draw the shape of what we are trying to say, we are defining the edges. Just as the event horizon telescopes define the black holes by what can be observed around them. The more data we can gather the clearer picture we can obtain on the question What happens when we sleep?

Philosopher Carl Jung dedicated much of his life to understanding the subconscious realms, seeking meaning and patterns in the chaos. He believed there are stories and archetypes that we share, he called this the 'collective unconscious'. Jung obviously did not have access to data in the same way we do today, I often wonder what he would have discovered if he was able to work with the tools we have today.

UNCONSCIOUS COLLECTIVE aims to explore patterns and find meaning in the dreams you submit, just like traditional science experiments the more data the clearer the understanding, so please submit as many times as you like and try to be precise in how you describe the dreams.





Dreamscapes



Some findings suggest that we experience different universes in our dreams and many of us have reoccuring dreams in the sense that we recognise the dream universe is one we have visited before even if the narrative of the dream is different.

On this platform we refer to these as Dreamscapes. I am very interested in trying to recreate these dreamscapes in our conscious reality usually they take the form of art.
